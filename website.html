overview

Mbti is a personality test that divides people into :





INFP , INFJ , INTP , INTJ , ISFP , ISFJ ISTJ , ISTP , ESFJ , ESFP , ESTJ , ESTP ENFJ , ENFP , ENTJ , ENTP Mbti was created by Myers and Briggs but was based on the difinition of cognitive functions first created by Carl Jung cognitive functions are our ways of making decisions (Judging functions) and preciving the world (Preciving functions) The Judging functions are : introverted thinking (Ti) , introverted feeling (Fi) , extroverted thinking (Te) , extroverted feeling (Fe). The preciving functions are : introverted intuition (Ni) , introverted sensing (Si) extroverted intuition (Ne) , extroverted sensing (Se).

the main functions:

1- the dominant function: it is the function a type uses most therefore it is the most well developed function of all. 2- the auxiliary function: it supports the dominant fuction , and provides balance for the dominant function. 3- the teritary function: typologists call this function often ' the eternal child' or 'the relief function'. it helps with handeling the real world altough the types generally don't prefer using it unless they absolutely have to. 4- the inferior function: it is the weakest function of a type , the development of it tends to come in late midlife and it's the least intresting to the types

the shadow functions:

they are the functions you are least aware of they can have positive and negative effects they should be embraced and not developed 5- the opposing role: it shows up as a defender from threats it is more passive-aggressive and it can feel lifeless to the types but it does defend well when it is embraced. 6- the critical parent: from the name of it , we know this function can be really critical ,it can be projected unto yourself or others. recognizing it and embracing what it has to offer can be really helpful with anxiety 7- the tricster function:
